Dosa

Description:

The Dosa is a South Indian delicacy. It's India's version of a pancake. Its made using a fermented batter. It is usually made very thin and crispy and served hot with sambar and chutney. There are many variations of this dish including things such as masala dosa or rava dosa.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flower
  • 2 tblsp vegetable oil
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 pinch baking soda
  • 1 3/4 cups water
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil

Directions:

  1. Combine flour, 2 tablespoons oil, salt, and baking soda together in a bowl.
  2. Mix for 1 to 2 minutes.
  3. Add 1 3/4 cup water and mix until it's lump free, the chunks of flour have dissolved, and the batter has a good consistency, adding more water if needed.
  4. Heat 1 tablespoon oil on a griddle over medium heat.
  5. Pour some of the batter onto the griddle; spread the batter in a circular motion, slowly yet steadily.
  6. Cook until the edges start to brown, 2 to 3 minutes.
  7. Remove from the grill.
  8. Repeat with remaining batter, adding more oil between each batch.
